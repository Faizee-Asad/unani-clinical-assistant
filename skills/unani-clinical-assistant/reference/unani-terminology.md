# Unani Terminology Reference

Use these terms carefully and educationally. Do not overstate certainty.

## Core concepts

- **Mizaj** — temperament/constitutional quality; commonly discussed as hot/cold and moist/dry patterns.
- **Akhlat** — humors; often described as Dam, Balgham, Safra, and Sauda in classical teaching.
- **Asbab-e-Sitta Zarooriya** — six essential factors often reviewed in lifestyle assessment.
- **Tabiyat / Quwwat-e-Mudabbira-e-Badan** — innate regulatory/defensive capacity, described educationally.
- **Sue Mizaj** — altered or imbalanced temperament concept.
- **Imtila** — concept of fullness/congestion in classical framing.
- **Istifragh** — evacuation/elimination concept.
- **Tadeel-e-Mizaj** — restoration/moderation of temperament.

## Asbab-e-Sitta Zarooriya checklist

1. Air/environment
2. Food and drink
3. Movement and rest
4. Sleep and wakefulness
5. Retention and evacuation
6. Mental/emotional states

## Preferred wording

- “Classical Unani framing could consider…”
- “Mizaj assessment requires trained clinical evaluation.”
- “Correlate with examination and investigations.”
- “Educational possibilities include…”

Avoid:

- “The patient definitely has…”
- “This proves…”
- “This will cure…”
