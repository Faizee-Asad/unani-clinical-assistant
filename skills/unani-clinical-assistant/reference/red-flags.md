# Red Flags and Referral Triggers

This list is not exhaustive. When unsure, advise assessment by a qualified clinician.

## General urgent red flags

- Chest pain, pressure, or pain radiating to arm/jaw/back
- Severe breathlessness or bluish lips
- Loss of consciousness, seizure, severe confusion
- Sudden weakness, facial droop, speech difficulty, vision loss
- Severe headache of sudden onset
- High fever with stiff neck, rash, confusion, or severe weakness
- Severe dehydration, persistent vomiting, inability to keep fluids
- Severe abdominal pain, rigid abdomen, blood in vomit/stool
- Uncontrolled bleeding
- Severe allergic reaction, facial/lip/tongue swelling, wheeze
- Severe injury, burns, fracture suspicion
- Poisoning, overdose, or suspected toxic ingestion
- Suicidal thoughts, self-harm, violent risk

## Gastrointestinal red flags

- Blood in stool or black tarry stool
- Unexplained weight loss
- Persistent vomiting
- Severe abdominal distension
- New constipation with severe pain or vomiting
- Jaundice
- Progressive difficulty swallowing

## Respiratory red flags

- Severe breathlessness
- Coughing blood
- Chest pain with breathing difficulty
- Fever with worsening respiratory distress

## Gynecology/obstetric red flags

- Heavy bleeding
- Pregnancy with abdominal pain or bleeding
- Severe headache or swelling in pregnancy
- Fever after delivery or miscarriage
- Foul-smelling discharge with fever

## Pediatric red flags

- Infant under 3 months with fever
- Lethargy, poor feeding, persistent vomiting
- Difficulty breathing
- Dehydration signs
- Seizure
- Non-blanching rash

## Medication/procedure safety red flags

- Known allergy or previous severe reaction
- Pregnancy/lactation with medicine request
- Liver/kidney disease
- Bleeding disorder or anticoagulants
- Multiple medicines with interaction risk
- Immunocompromised state
