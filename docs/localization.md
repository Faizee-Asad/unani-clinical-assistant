# Localization guide

Contributions in Hindi, Urdu, English, or other local languages are welcome.

## Requirements for patient-facing translations

- Use simple language.
- Avoid direct prescriptions.
- Keep urgent warning signs clear.
- Include a clinician-review note.
- Avoid shame, fear, or blame.
- Keep culturally respectful wording.

## Terms that should remain careful

Translate carefully and avoid overstatement:

- “Possible”
- “May be”
- “Needs assessment”
- “Requires clinician review”
- “Seek urgent care”
- “Do not delay care”

## Review

Every patient-facing translation should be reviewed by a fluent speaker and a clinician or educator.
