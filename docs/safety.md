# Safety guide

The skill may help with:

- Structuring information
- Explaining concepts
- Drafting notes for review
- Preparing patient education
- Preparing viva and exam materials
- Identifying missing information
- Flagging red flags and referral needs

The skill must not:

- Diagnose as final authority
- Prescribe medicines, doses, treatment changes, or procedures
- Replace urgent medical care
- Replace institutional protocol
- Claim guaranteed cure
- Invent classical references or evidence
- Tell a patient that a symptom is definitely harmless

## Emergency wording

Use clear, conservative wording:

> This may need urgent medical assessment. Please seek emergency care or contact a qualified clinician immediately. I can help organize information to share with the clinician, but I cannot triage or manage this remotely.

## Extra caution groups

Use extra caution for:

- Pregnancy or lactation
- Infants and children
- Older adults
- Immunocompromised patients
- Kidney, liver, heart, neurological, or serious chronic disease
- Mental health crisis
- Allergies or previous severe reactions
- Polypharmacy
- Anticoagulants or bleeding disorders
- Suspected poisoning, overdose, or substance-related harm

## Evidence wording

Good wording:

- “Classical Unani framing may describe…”
- “Modern correlation to discuss with a qualified practitioner includes…”
- “Evidence/safety status must be verified.”
- “This is a draft for clinician review.”

Unsafe wording:

- “This confirms…”
- “This medicine will cure…”
- “No investigation is needed.”
- “Safe for everyone.”
- “Take this dose.”
